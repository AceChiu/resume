I am Chiu Yu-Wei. I have worked in the office of the WT microelectronics Co., Ltd. since 2016. I am the MIS Engineer in Architecture Department. I am responsible for Back-End java development for B2B data exchange via Rosettanet/EDI protocol. I have handled on many B2B transaction for venders and customers, such as Broadcom, NXP, Asus, and Quanta. To elaborate, I implement new B2B transaction service in new system and conduct code refactoring for inefficient and cluttered codes in old systems. I used to spend time and effort on modifying when the business transaction was changed in old systems. As a result, I provide some web services to let our users modify by themselves. Successfully, I save time on maintaining significantly. That is to say, I increase efficiency for the company. In addition, I designed and built ETL (Extract-Transform-Load) system by SAP Data service. The system extracts the data used in Business Intelligence. This innovative system indeed benefits my company.

After work, I seize opportunities to hone my programming skills. I collaborated with my friend to create a purchase, sales and inventory management system for an e-commerce company. The system shortens the time and manpower needed for the inner process of enterprise. Additionally, according to Stack Overflow’s Annual Survey of 2018, JavaScript is the most common programming language. I developed a quantitative analysis program which can alert users of irregular movements in Taiwan's Stock Market by Node.js, D3js and Express Framework. Apparently, I am not only professional in Java but also well-experienced in JavaScript/Node.js.

When I majored in Physics, I was interested in computer science. I learned C Programming Language spontaneously, and I created a simulated vending machine by Verilog in Digital Logic Design. After I got a bachelor's degree, I pursued Master of Photonics and specialized in semiconductor. In my master's thesis, I utilized Mathlab to simulate optical properties. In the second year of the Master's degree, my father was diagnosed with lung cancer. In the first two months of he death, I took care of him and took a leave of absence. During this time, I thought about what is my future. Everyone should pursue it which we like most during our life. I want to be a software engineer. After my father's funeral, I came back to school and review C. Two months later, I didn't know how could I make a project by C. At this time, my senior who was computer science PH.D suggested me learn Java Programming Language and build a weblog as my project. Also, I learned Operator System and Data Structure spontaneously because he said it's the fundamental of computer science. Duo to the learning process, I could get the job of the WT microelectronics Co., Ltd.

My supervisor attaches great importance to clean code and we pay attention to readable and testable code in our project; moreover, I spend ten hours studying new skill every week. Generally, I am capable and competent in the job. Also, I am honest, flexible and easy-going. It seems to me that we will develop a close rapport with co-workers. In my spare time, I love playing guitar, hiking, surfing and diving. I plan to take the advanced diving license in 2019. In conclusion, hoping I am closely suited to the position requirement.
